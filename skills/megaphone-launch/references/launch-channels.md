# Launch channels reference

Specs and submission rules for each channel Megaphone supports. Re-read this rather than relying on memory — channel rules change.

Last updated: April 2026.

---

## Product Hunt

- **Best launch days:** Tuesday or Wednesday at 12:01 AM PST. Avoid Mondays (low traffic) and Fridays (rolls into the weekend).
- **Tagline:** ≤60 characters. Names the audience and the outcome.
- **Description:** ≤260 characters. Expand the tagline; end with a CTA.
- **Gallery:** 4–6 images recommended. First image is the most important — make it a clean screenshot or hero shot, not a logo.
- **Topics:** Pick 3 relevant topics; affects discovery on PH's category pages.
- **Maker comment:** Post one immediately at launch. Format: short story of why you built it, what's specifically interesting about it, what feedback you're looking for.
- **Threshold for top placement (2026):** Top 5 of the day typically requires 300–600 upvotes; #1 requires 500–1,200. Engagement signals (comments, time-on-page, maker replies) matter more than raw upvotes in 2026's algorithm.
- **Pre-launch:** A "ship" page can be set up before launch to collect notifications. Use it.

---

## Hacker News (Show HN)

- **Submission:** must be by a real human account with non-trivial karma. No bot submissions, no API auto-posts.
- **Title format:** `Show HN: <project> – <one-liner>`. Example: `Show HN: Megaphone – Distribution toolkit for vibe coders`.
- **Description (the first comment by the maker):** explain the technical interesting bits, not the marketing pitch. HN responds to "here's a hard problem we solved" much better than "here's a beautiful product."
- **Best time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9 AM PST. Posts before 6 AM get buried; afternoons rarely make the front page.
- **Tone:** technical, modest, link-heavy. Avoid superlatives. Be ready to defend choices in the comments.
- **Bait:** never use "AI-powered" / "revolutionary" / "10x" / "the future of X" — HN's commenters will roast a project for these alone.

## BetaList

- **Wait time:** typically 4 weeks for the free tier; paid options speed this up.
- **Audience:** early-adopter / waitlist signups, not stargazers.
- **Best fit:** pre-launch products with a landing page and email capture.
- **Submission:** tagline, description, screenshot, signup link.
- **Outcome to expect:** a few hundred email signups for solid products.

---

## Peerlist Launchpad

- **Cadence:** weekly. Submissions go into the next week's Launchpad batch.
- **Submission:** project description, problem/solution, demo URL, GitHub link if applicable.
- **Audience:** indie makers, designers, builders. Forum-style feedback culture.
- **Side benefit:** Peerlist profiles double as a portfolio with a DR-75 backlink.

---

## DevHunt

- **Audience:** developer tools, APIs, SDKs, libraries.
- **Submission:** free tier has a wait of 6 weeks; $49 buys next-week placement.
- **Tagline / description:** developer-tone, technical specifics over marketing fluff.
- **Side benefit:** has a "Product Launch List" feature that auto-submits to 100+ directories in one click — worth using as a follow-up after the main launch.

---

## TinyLaunch / Smol Launch / Open Launch

- **Audience:** indie projects, side projects, early-stage startups.
- **Submission:** free; lightweight forms with tagline, screenshot, link.
- **Cadence:** weekly leaderboards. Top picks get badges that double as social proof.
- **Best as:** complementary submissions on launch day after the main PH push.

---

## Indie Hackers

- **Best post types:** "I just launched X" with a screenshot and a question, or "I built X in <timeframe> for <reason>" — story-shaped.
- **Tone:** founder-to-founder, transparent about numbers and process.
- **No hard limits on post length;** 400–800 words is the sweet spot.
- **Engagement:** reply to every comment for the first 24 hours. The community values that.

---

## dev.to

- **Best post types:** technical walkthroughs ("How I built X with Y"), retrospectives, comparison posts.
- **Length:** 800+ words for best ranking; 1500–2500 hits the algorithm sweet spot.
- **Tags:** 4 tags max. Use the project's stack as tags (e.g., `#javascript`, `#react`, `#typescript`).
- **Canonical URL:** if cross-posting from a personal blog, set canonical to the original.
- **Cover image:** strongly recommended. Free tools: Canva, Figma export, or just a clean screenshot.

---

## Reddit (subreddit-specific)

- **Always check the rules.** Many subs ban self-promotion, link posts, or AI-generated content.
- **Disclose maker status** in the post body. Reddit hates stealth marketing more than honest promotion.
- **Preferred shape:** self-post (text post) with a link in the body, not a link post.
- **Title:** does not need "Show", does not need brand voice. State plainly what it is and what's interesting.
- **Best subs to consider** (always verify rules first):
  - `r/SideProject` — explicitly accepts maker promotion
  - `r/InternetIsBeautiful` — for visually distinctive web projects
  - `r/programming` — for substantive technical OSS only; will downvote anything fluffy
  - `r/webdev`, `r/reactjs`, `r/Python`, etc. — language/framework-specific
  - `r/SaaS`, `r/Entrepreneur` — for B2B products, with caveats around self-promo rules

## Bluesky

- **Char limit:** 300.
- **Tone:** developer-friendly, casual, less polished than LinkedIn but more substantive than X.
- **No hashtags by convention** (Bluesky culture does not use them the way Mastodon or LinkedIn does).
- **Best post shape:** opening hook in the first line, one concrete detail, link last. Threads work but singletons are also fine.

---

## LinkedIn

- **Char limit:** 3000, but algorithmic sweet spot is 1200–2000.
- **Tone:** more polished, founder-narrative friendly. Less casual than Bluesky.
- **Best post shape:** lead with a specific moment / lesson, walk through the takeaway, end with a question. Posts that ask a question get 2–3× the comment engagement.
- **Don't use:** "I'm excited to announce", "I'm thrilled", or any humblebrag opener. The platform tolerates these but readers tune them out.

---

## X (Twitter)

- **API status (Feb 2026):** free tier removed; pay-per-use ~$0.01/post created. Bot accounts must self-disclose in bio. AI-generated replies require explicit X approval.
- **Char limit:** 280, or thread (preferred for build-in-public).
- **Best post shape:** thread with hook in tweet 1, body in 3–5 tweets, link in the last. Singletons can work but threads outperform for project launches.
- **Best time:** 8–10 AM ET, Tuesday–Thursday. Avoid weekends.
- **For Megaphone:** treat as draft-only by default. If the user wants automated posting, they bring their own X dev key and accept the cost / disclosure.

---

## Threads

- **Char limit:** 500.
- **Tone:** casual, more visual. Lower bar for polish than LinkedIn or dev.to.
- **API:** Meta's Threads API is available; usable through Post for Me, Ayrshare, etc.
- **Audience:** still skewing more "general consumer" than developer; best for end-user-facing products.

---

## Mastodon

- **Char limit:** 500 default (varies by instance).
- **Tone:** friendly, hashtag-positive (unlike Bluesky).
- **Best instances for indie tech:** mastodon.social, fosstodon.org (FOSS-focused), hachyderm.io (tech-focused).
- **Hashtags work:** add 2–4 relevant ones; that's how Mastodon's federated discovery happens.
